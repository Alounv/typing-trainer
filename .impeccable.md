# Typing Trainer

## Design Context

### Users

Solo typists who want to improve. They show up to a focused tool, run a short
drill, and leave. Context: a quiet desk, often dark room, alone. They're not
casual; they're the kind of user who checks their WPM.

The product's job is to measure, prescribe, and track progress — not to
gamify or entertain.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **disciplined**, **understated**, **precise**.

The voice is that of a well-made instrument. A mechanical watch's dial: you
can read it in a glance, it tells you only what you need, and every mark is
where it is for a reason.

### Aesthetic Direction

Minimal, text-first, typographically disciplined. The existing design language
is already well-formed — the craft job is to sustain it, not invent new
motifs. Observed patterns:

- **Typography**: inter (sans-serif) for UI; numbers set in `font-mono` with
  `tabular-nums` for alignment. Hierarchy from size + weight, not color.
- **Micro-labels**: `tracking-[0.18em] uppercase text-xs font-medium` in
  `text-base-content/50`. Used for section numbers (`01`, `02`, `03`) and
  contextual keys (`STEP 1`, `TYPING TRAINER · V0.1`).
- **Dividers**: hairline `border-base-300`. Sections separated by rules, not
  cards or shadows.
- **Color**: daisyUI themes. Primary reserved for active state and progress.
  Neutrals do almost all the work. Text uses opacity stops
  (`text-base-content/90, /80, /70, /65, /55, /50, /45, /40, /35`).
- **Chips**: `rounded-sm bg-base-200 px-2 py-0.5 font-mono text-xs
text-base-content/80`. Used for bigram targets on the dashboard plan card.
- **Prose**: `max-w-xl text-sm text-base-content/65` for instructional text.
- No shadows, no gradients, no decorative icons, no rounded-card layouts.

### Design Principles

1. **Instruments, not apps.** Every screen looks like a control panel for a
   precise tool. Information, then action.
2. **Hierarchy by typography, not by container.** The app rarely wraps things
   in cards. Rules, spacing, and opacity do the work.
3. **Numbers are first-class citizens.** Monospace + tabular-nums for every
   figure. Numbers should align like they would on a lab sheet.
4. **Copy is direct.** "Today's plan." "Start →." "Reset to defaults." No
   marketing fluff, no emoji, no exclamation points.
5. **Micro-labels over headings for supporting content.** Uppercase,
   letter-spaced, small, low-contrast — used to classify adjacent content
   without competing for attention.
